City Fiscal Dashboard

Find your fiscal peers

How city leaders confront revenue and spending decisions is influenced by the financial, economic and political variables at work in their communities. Cities that have similar abilities to adjust property or income taxes will have more in common on financial matters than cities that are alike in other ways, like population or geography. But knowing which cities share a similar financial ecosystem can be difficult.

To help city leaders identify their financial “peers” and benchmark their own progress, we’ve used the Fiscal Policy Space (FPS) framework devised by the College of Urban Planning and Public Affairs at the University of Illinois at Chicago to analyze 100 cities across four factors:

  • Fiscal Base Score indicates a city’s revenue capacity and is based on the average of property, sales and income tax collections per capita
  • Tax Authority Score shows a city’s ability to collect tax revenue per state statute and as demonstrated by the type or types of taxes a city collects (property, sales, and income tax)
  • Property Tax Gap is a ratio describing how much a city uses property tax as a source of revenue by comparing the maximum property tax revenue state statute allows to the actual amount collected
  • Demand Score rates a city’s demand for local government services and political culture based on the average of income, political ideology and public sector unionization

Using a cluster analysis, we’ve formed four peer groups to facilitate fiscal benchmarking. Use the map below to see how your city compares.
